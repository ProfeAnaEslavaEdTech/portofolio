The corpus at a glance

Each subcorpus concatenates 30 abstracts into a standardised 3,000-word text. METOOL reports types and tokens per subcorpus; the Type–Token Ratio (TTR) indexes lexical density — all six subcorpora fall in the low band (0.319–0.339), confirming comparable lexical texture across disciplines. Token counts exceed 3,000 because METOOL counts reformulation punctuation as tokens.

    Section 4

    Sampling population & access

    The sampling frame comprises 4,049 TFMs deposited across the six knowledge areas in 2022–2024, split almost evenly into open-access and restricted (closed) theses. From this frame, 30 abstracts per subcorpus were sampled on rhetorical criteria. Abstracts of restricted theses were read from the RiuNet preview; the rest from the final director-approved PDF.

    Section 5

    Method notes & limitations

    Disclosed for transparency, drawn directly from the workbook's Data Dictionary and Limitations draft.

    Textual (interactive) — Logical markers, Code Glosses, Frame markers, Endophoric markers, Evidentials. These organise the discourse and guide the reader through the text.

    Interpersonal (interactional) — Boosters, Hedges, Attitude markers, Engagement markers, Self-mentions. These project the writer's stance and involve the reader.

    Markers were annotated with METOOL. Polyfunctional forms (e.g. and, will, such as) are counted once per function under the double-classification protocol, so a form can appear in more than one category.

    Each subcorpus contains 30 abstracts, 10 per deposit year (2022–2024), ~1,000 words per year, selected on rhetorical criteria (lexical bundles signalling Moves 2–4; at least three of five moves per abstract).

    Ten fine-grained subcorpora were originally collected; in consultation with the thesis supervisor the design was consolidated to the six established knowledge areas. Within Engineering & Architecture, only Architecture was kept separate, on the grounds of its distinct, design-oriented rhetorical culture.

    The Health Sciences subcorpus deviates from the balanced 10/10/10 year design (final distribution 10/12/8): 2024 did not offer enough eligible TFMs, so two records were drawn from 2023 to reach the 30-abstract target.

    Because the year groups are unequal, per-year marker rates in this subcorpus cannot be compared on equal terms and the diachronic analysis was postponed. This does not affect the cross-disciplinary comparison, which rests on the complete 3,000-word subcorpora.

    Architecture duplicate. The 2022 record IAAQ7 was inadvertently duplicated into a 2023 slot (+74 non-unique words, +2.5%). Sensitivity analysis: shift <0.2 per 1,000 tokens; no between-discipline comparison affected; the duplicate is excluded from per-abstract and diachronic analyses.

    Source flags. Three abstracts (ENG-20, STH-09, SCI-30) lacked a source flag; all three were confirmed as extracted from the final director-approved TFM PDF and coded accordingly.

    Gender coding. Author gender was coded from given names in the Spanish naming context; 16 records (8.9%), mostly in Arts & Humanities, could not be reliably attributed and were left blank. Any gender comparison is exploratory.

    All 180 abstracts come from a single institutional repository (RiuNet, UPV) and represent novice academic writing in English by L1-Spanish master's students; generalisation requires multi-institutional replication, and expert-corpus comparisons read as novice-vs-expert contrasts.

    With 3,000 words per subcorpus, low-frequency categories (e.g. endophoric markers, 3–5 occurrences) yield cells too small for inferential treatment and are reported descriptively only. METOOL's automated sense assignment carries an inherent misclassification risk that systematic manual review mitigated but did not eliminate.
