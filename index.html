Course Description

I developed this six-level gamified course to help undergraduate and master's students strengthen their academic English and research communication skills. The course guides learners through a progressive journey from academic vocabulary development to reading, analysing, writing, and presenting research abstracts. Drawing on Swales' Move Analysis and Hyland's Metadiscourse Framework, learners gradually build the linguistic and rhetorical skills required for academic success in English-medium higher education.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, learners can read research abstracts critically, identify rhetorical structures and metadiscourse strategies, write a cohesive academic abstract, and deliver a concise spoken research summary with confidence.

3 · Second Language Acquisition

SLA Principles in Practice

Comprehensible Input +1 (Krashen)

Resources are pitched at i+1, scaffolded by video, infographics, and AI support — always slightly above current proficiency.

Noticing Hypothesis (Schmidt)

Level 3 metadiscourse marker activities make implicit grammatical features salient through focused attention tasks.

Output Hypothesis (Swain)

Levels 5–6 require learners to produce a full abstract and a spoken conference pitch, triggering syntactic processing.

Swales' 5-Move Structure

Core academic writing framework introduced in Level 2, applied progressively through Levels 3–5 using real research abstracts.

Hyland's Metadiscourse Framework

Interactive (frame markers, transitions, evidentials) and Interactional markers (hedges, boosters, attitude markers) taught explicitly in Level 3 and applied in Level 4 through a Hyland Taxonomy game.

4 · eLearning & UX Design

Inclusive Design Decisions

🧠

Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller)

Information chunked into 6 levels × 3 activities. Collapsible sections, progressive disclosure, and clean visual hierarchy prevent overload.

♿

Universal Design for Learning (CAST)

Floating UDL bar: voice dictation (Web Speech API), large text, high-contrast mode, read-back — multiple means of representation and action.

🎬

Multimodal Learning

Video (YouTube/TED-Ed), interactive Canva resources, AI tools (GPT), peer platforms (Padlet, Flip), and audio tools (Vocaroo, Synthesia).

5 · Responsible Human–AI Collaboration

AI Literacy in Educational Design

This project was developed with Claude Code (Anthropic) as AI-Powered Web Development Engineer — a deliberate methodological choice to model and document ethical, transparent, and pedagogically-grounded Human–AI collaboration in educational design.

The AI was used as a technical co-author, not a replacement for the Subject Matter Expert's pedagogical decision-making. All instructional design decisions, content curation, and learning objectives were authored by the human educator.
