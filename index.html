Academic Writing Journey: From Reader to Research Communicator

Major / Minor

English Language Skills (B2→C1) · Minor: Academic Literacy

Format

1 Canva Course · 18 sequenced Activities · Fully async, self-paced

Audience

Undergraduate (Years 2–4) & Master's students (Years 1–2)

Course description: A six-level gamified course helping undergraduate and master's students strengthen academic English and research communication skills — from vocabulary development to reading, analysing, writing, and presenting research abstracts, drawing on Swales' Move Analysis and Hyland's Metadiscourse Framework.

Learning outcomes: By course end, learners can read research abstracts critically, identify rhetorical structures and metadiscourse strategies, write a cohesive academic abstract, and deliver a concise spoken research summary with confidence.

Comprehensible Input +1 (Krashen)

Resources pitched at i+1, scaffolded by video, infographics, and AI support — always slightly above current proficiency.

Noticing Hypothesis (Schmidt)

Level 3 metadiscourse marker activities make implicit grammatical features salient through focused attention tasks.

Output Hypothesis (Swain)

Levels 5–6 require producing a full abstract and a spoken conference pitch, triggering syntactic processing.

Swales' 5-Move Structure

Core academic writing framework introduced in Level 2, applied progressively through Levels 3–5 using real research abstracts.

Hyland's Metadiscourse Framework

Interactive and Interactional markers taught explicitly in Level 3 and applied in Level 4 through a Hyland Taxonomy game.

🧠

Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller)

Information chunked into 6 levels × 3 activities. Collapsible sections, progressive disclosure, and clean visual hierarchy prevent overload.

♿

Universal Design for Learning (CAST)

Floating UDL bar: voice dictation (Web Speech API), large text, high-contrast mode, read-back — multiple means of representation and action.

🎬

Multimodal Learning

Video (YouTube/TED-Ed), interactive Canva resources, AI tools, peer platforms (Padlet, Flip), and audio tools (Vocaroo, Synthesia).
